Idea

The Cosmeticon 2023 project focuses on designing a landing page for a beauty and cosmetics event, with the goal of clearly presenting information while maintaining a strong and engaging visual identity.

Events in the beauty industry often rely heavily on visual appeal, but that can come at the cost of clarity and usability. This project explores how to balance expressive visuals with a structured layout that allows users to quickly understand what the event is about, who is participating, and how to take part.

The main purpose of the design is to guide users naturally through the page, highlighting speakers, attractions, and schedules, and leading them toward ticket purchase without overwhelming them with information.

Research

The first step of the project was understanding the needs of an event landing page and how users typically interact with this type of content.

Research focused on identifying common patterns used in event websites, especially within the beauty and fashion industries, where strong branding and visual storytelling play a key role.

From this analysis, the project emphasized clear content hierarchy, strong calls to action, and a layout that supports both exploration and quick decision-making.

Wireframing

The wireframing phase focused on defining the structure of the landing page and organizing content in a way that feels intuitive and easy to scan.

The initial layout was designed to make the most important information immediately visible, while allowing users to scroll naturally through additional details.

Key sections defined at this stage included:

  • hero section with event branding and key information
  • featured speakers and attractions
  • event schedule and highlights
  • ticket information and call-to-action sections

This structure helped ensure users could understand the event and its value within the first few scrolls.

Special attention was given to how information density could be managed without compromising the visual impact of the page.

Content blocks were designed to be visually distinct, allowing users to quickly differentiate between sections while maintaining a cohesive flow.

Information was grouped to support readability, focusing on:

  • clear separation between informational and promotional content
  • consistent spacing and alignment
  • strong visual cues for calls to action

The design was then adapted with responsiveness in mind, ensuring that the same content structure could be effectively displayed across different screen sizes.

Layout decisions were tested to preserve hierarchy and readability, especially on smaller screens.

Particular care was taken to ensure that calls to action remained visible and accessible throughout the experience.

This allowed users to engage with the event and proceed to ticket purchase at any point in their navigation flow.
