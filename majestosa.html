Idea

Majestosa is a branding project developed for a beer sold at the Heriberto Hülse stadium bar, created to represent the identity, history, and symbolism of Criciúma Esporte Clube and its supporters.

This project is deeply personal. Criciúma is my hometown, and I've been a fan of the team ever since I figured out what football was, this work reflects not only graphic exploration, but also a genuine emotional connection to the club, the city, and its fan culture. The identity is informed by lived experience — from match days to the visual language that surrounds the club year after year.

The goal was to translate the intensity of match days, the pride of the fan base, and the club’s visual legacy into a bold and memorable graphic identity. Rather than creating a neutral or generic product, the intention was to design something that feels authentic to the supporters and rooted in local culture.

Briefing

The beer was designed to be sold in the premises of the Heriberto Hülse stadium (not inside the stadium itself as cans and bottles are forbidden as per Brazillian law), representing the fan base and iconography of Criciúma Esporte Clube.

The identity needed to reflect the club’s history, its visual symbols, and the strong relationship it shares with its supporters — especially the Torcida Carvoeira.

Target Audience

Fans aged 18 and above, regardless of their level of involvement in organized supporter groups.

The identity was designed to resonate both with long-time supporters and casual fans who share a connection with the club and the city.

Typography

A custom typeface was created specifically for this project, inspired by the presence and energy of the Carvoeira fan base inside the stadium.

The goal was to design a typeface that feels strong, imposing, and memorable — echoing the atmosphere of match days at Heriberto Hülse.

The result is TIGRE, a bold serif typeface with tight spacing, designed to convey impact and tradition.

Icon & Symbolism

The main icon was developed from an aerial outline of the Heriberto Hülse stadium — the club’s most important physical and symbolic landmark.

The shape preserves prominent stadium features such as angular corners, covered grandstands, and the moat separating the field — a defining characteristic of the stadium.

A charcoal-textured mask was applied to reference charcoal paintings, a cultural element strongly associated with the city of Criciúma and its history.

Colors & Variations

The color palette is based on black, white, and yellow — colors historically associated with the club since 1984.

The order and contrast of these colors directly reference the classic team uniform, reinforcing recognition and emotional connection.

Multiple icon color variations were created to ensure flexibility across different backgrounds and applications.

Three stars were added to the identity, representing the club’s national titles: the 1991 Copa do Brasil, the 2002 Série B, and the 2006 Série C.

Applications

The identity was applied to beer cans and labels, with different logo configurations designed for various use cases.

A simplified version of the logo was created for smaller or more constrained spaces, while a complete version — including icon, wordmark, slogan, and stars — was reserved for primary applications.

These applications demonstrate how the identity adapts while maintaining consistency and impact.
